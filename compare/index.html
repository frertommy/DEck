Notch · Market sizing

Persistent derivatives dwarf their predecessors. Every time.

The pattern is universal. Wherever a persistent, leveraged instrument got built on top of an existing options-like or expiring-bet market, the new instrument ended up multiples larger than its predecessor — usually within a decade. Sports is the only major asset class where this hasn't happened yet.

02 · Options → Persistent derivative
Rates · 1998–2022
Interest rate options vs. interest rate swaps
7–8×today
OTC notional outstanding, USD trillions. First IRS: IBM/World Bank, 1981.
IR options (caps, floors, swaptions) Interest rate swaps
In the late 70s, the only way to express a rate view was through expiring options. The swap was invented in 1981 and gave you multi-year persistent exposure instead of expiring optionality. Today IRS is the largest derivative market on earth at $380T+ notional. The optionality version stalled below $55T.
Equities · 2000–2024
S&P 500 options vs. S&P 500 futures
~10×daily volume
Annual notional volume, USD trillions. SPX options launched 1983; ES e-mini futures 1997.
SPX options ES futures
Both exist on the same underlying, both available to the same traders, both have been around for decades. ES daily notional runs roughly 10× SPX options. CME's own data: ES has 8× the liquidity of all S&P 500 ETFs combined.
Implication for Notch

Sports betting and prediction markets are the options market for sports views — fixed-odds bets and binary YES/NO contracts are structurally digital options on real-world events. We are the persistent derivative layer that doesn't exist yet.

Across rates, credit, crypto, and equities, the persistent instrument ended up multiples larger than what came before — often 10× or more, usually within a decade. Sports has a 3.5B-fan audience, $500B+ in expiring bets, and zero persistent-derivative infrastructure. It's the largest unfinanced asset class on earth.
