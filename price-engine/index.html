The Core Idea

Every team has a perpetual price. Result-driven base, market-driven overlay, live in-play layer. Three things stacked on top of each other, nothing else.

Problem: Sports betting expires. Every market — sportsbook, prediction-market, fantasy — resets to zero after each match. There's no persistent instrument that lets a fan or trader take a continuous position on a team's strength across a season.

Solution: A perpetual price per team, anchored to bookmaker consensus, refined by results, smoothed across the calendar. Arsenal at $298.30 means the market rates them in the elite tier. After a 0-2 loss to Manchester City, the price drops to roughly $287.78. After they beat Newcastle 2-1, it climbs back. The instrument never expires.

Why it works: The global betting market reprices every football fixture continuously. JOJO converts that liquidity into a continuous pricing signal — using a transparent decomposition that lets traders see exactly which component moved, when, and why. Conservation is mechanical, not statistical: at every state transition, pre-event price equals post-event price.

① arch_b · result base

Updates only at settle. ΔB = K × (S − E_KR) where S is the realized outcome (1 / 0.5 / 0) and E_KR is the consensus expected score locked at kickoff. Permanent. The "earnings" of a team's price.

② arch_m1 · market overlay

Drifts continuously between matches via Option-K absorption from market repricing on the next-next fixture. Acts as a slow-moving consensus correction layer. Bounded by ±M1_CLAMP for safety.

③ L · live layer

Active only during the 90 minutes. Updates every 2-5 seconds from in-play bookmaker odds. Resets at settle. The reason the chart visibly responds to goals, red cards, and momentum swings in real time.

Conservation invariant

At settle: arch_b += ΔB · arch_m1 += (L_FT − ΔB). The residual is mechanically absorbed into arch_m1, so the published price is continuous across the settle event.

① Pre-match · continuous

Bookmakers price the fixture starting ~14 days before kickoff. arch_m1 absorbs market drift continuously. Pre-match outcome projections become available the moment any book publishes lines.

② Kickoff · E_KR locks

At KO, the consensus expected-score snapshots into the oracle. This locks the result formula's pivot. ΔB(S, E_KR) is now deterministic for each S ∈ {0, 0.5, 1}.

③ In-play · L drives the chart

For 90+ minutes, L responds tick-by-tick to live odds. Goals push L up or down sharply. Red cards bend the trajectory. arch_b and arch_m1 stay frozen — visible chart movement during this phase is L alone.

④ Settle · atomic conservation

S becomes truth. ΔB applies to arch_b. (L_FT − ΔB) absorbs into arch_m1. L resets. Pre-settle published price equals post-settle published price by construction.

Design Principles

Four principles that make the engine state stable, predictable, and immune to common pricing-oracle failure modes.

PRINCIPLE01
Result-anchored state · solver-decoupled

JOJO uses a Bradley-Terry solver as a reference signal, not as authority over engine state. Solver convergence drift, iteration noise, and intermediate re-fits never propagate into the published price. The only things that modify state are realized match results (through ΔB) and continuous market drift on upcoming fixtures (through Option-K). This separation means JOJO's price evolution is deterministic and inspectable — every state change has a single, identifiable economic cause.

PRINCIPLE02
Conservation at every event boundary

At settlement, the engine doesn't compute a "best new price" — it conserves. arch_b += ΔB, then arch_m1 += (L_FT − ΔB). The pre-settle published price equals the post-settle published price by construction. No optimization, no smoothing, no estimation. Mechanical equality. Verified across 191 settlements. This is what allows JOJO to give traders a hard pre-match contract: "if your team wins, settle at X; if draw, Y; if loss, Z" — the numbers are guaranteed by the architecture, not promised by a model.

PRINCIPLE03
Settlement-time hygiene · two-condition certainty

When a result is final, JOJO snaps the live-odds input on the realized outcome before computing the FT layer. Two conditions must both hold: (a) the engine has confirmed the settle event, and (b) the market itself shows ≥95% on the same outcome at the final tick. Both engine and market agreeing the result is decided is what licenses cleaning the residual 1-5% of bookmaker spread that otherwise leaks noise into the L_FT computation. Median residual on converged matches drops to zero. Coverage: ~64% of all settlements.

PRINCIPLE04
Cluster-aware peer settlement

Saturday afternoons in the EPL produce clusters of 60+ teams settling within a 5-minute window. Naive designs propagate every peer-team's solver shift into every other team's state, producing correlated artifacts across an entire league at once. JOJO routes peer-cluster shifts through a separate accumulator and releases them cleanly at each team's own next settlement — never mixing peer-cluster turbulence into the active in-play state. Maximum release magnitude bounded under one dollar at the league's busiest moments.

What this buys you

Together these principles give traders something rare in oracle-anchored markets: a price they can predict before the match and a chart that only moves for reasons they can name. Every dollar of price movement maps to a result, a market drift, or a goal — never to solver iterations, settlement timing, or peer-team noise. That's the foundation a perpetual instrument needs.

A surprising empirical finding

E_KR doesn't tighten meaningfully in the last 24 hours before kickoff. Median projection error is roughly constant from 14 days out to 4 hours out — about $1 median. Bookmaker consensus is essentially set 1-2 weeks ahead. The projection card displays the same numbers for a week with high stability — turning the projection into a stable forward forecast, not an estimate that sharpens toward KO.
