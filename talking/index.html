The 6 fixes
  1. Cut the hedge stack 76×
    "Kind of / essentially / sort of / you know" make you sound unsure of your own product. You're not kind-of-first. You're first.
    we're kind of the first ones, essentially building a new asset class
    →we're first. we're building a new asset class.
  2. One idea per sentence
    Yours spiral and restart — you open a clause, abandon it, reopen. Short declaratives. Full stops. Biggest single upgrade to sounding sharp.
    this is 200x more capital efficient and could be a massive way to— jump in and affect it
    →it's 200x more capital efficient. that's how you take the market.
  3. Lead with the punch, don't trail off
    Strongest line first; stop on it. Your best material gets buried mid-ramble and your answers die on "yeah, that's kind of it" / "things like that."
    Open the liquidity answer with the 200–300k stat — don't arrive at it three sentences in.
  4. Pause on the numbers — they're your weapon
    $23T · 200x · 3–4% median · 40 books. You rattle and hedge them ("like 200, 300k"). Say the number. Stop. Then the implication. Numbers land in silence.
  5. Cut the apology "I think" ×12
    Never call your moat confusing or your user stupid ("formulas would confuse you," "very stupidly"). Drop reflexive "I think" — it turns assertions into opinions.
    I think that's the core of it→that's the core.
  6. Enumerate on purpose
    Your clearest stretch was the "number one… number two… third thing" answer — structure forced you crisp. Pre-load moat / GTM / why-now as "Three things. One, two, three."
Do not touch

The "I've been liquidated many times, I'll come back, it's an amazing experience" energy. Real, likable, sells the degen appeal to exactly that room. Your problem was never conviction. It's that precision leaks out through the fillers. Plug the leaks, keep the fire.

It's one move, not seven: when you'd reach for filler — pause instead.
The 10-minute daily loop
  1. Read aloud, recorded. ~5 min — Run the drill cards below out loud. At every ⊘: stop dead, breathe, continue. Record every rep. You can't fix what you can't hear.
  2. Count every filler. Log it. ~1 min — Not just "right?" — kind of, essentially, just, I think, all of it. One number a day. The number dropping is what rewires it.
  3. One real conversation, on purpose. live — Standup, a founder call, ordering coffee. Run the pause when it's real. Drilling alone doesn't transfer until you do.
  4. One slow rep. ~2 min — Read a card at deliberately-slow pace. Filler spikes when you rush; slowing gives your brain the beat it was stealing.
  5. Land the period. all reps — End sentences on a downward tone + full stop. Filler rides a rising, agreement-begging note. Statement, not invitation.
